BDLA Season I.

In order to build presence, we wanted eye-catching apparel with an iconic logo. When drafting ideas, a recurring theme was a basketball with a knife, influenced by American rapper 21 Savage's famous remark: "issa knife."
Sketches by Justin Kadi and Eric Kim.

The knife in the ball, an integral part of the logo design concept, is an antithesis to the form of a backstabber. The logo went through several iterations in Photoshop.

We wanted the logo to convey a urban, scrappy personality. As a result, the logo features sporadic paint splatter and the text "BDLA" in the grungy font Dry Brush, with LA underlined as the association originated in the Los Angeles area. (The "BD" being my initials is a nice plus.)

Apparel choice and logo placement took the majority of the time. Hockey jerseys or long sleeves? Long sleeves or crewnecks? Small logo or big? Black shirt or white? Or gray? Or... red? Iteration after constant iteration. Having 14 members and no clear voting system didn't help the cause.

After deciding on a big logo on a light gray crewneck sweatshirt, we wanted to give each of our 14 members an identity by adding customized text to the back of each crewneck. We went to a spreadsheet and decided on a name for each BDLA member. While names were being decided, we also voted on the font choice for our titles. Fifteen more iterations for fifteen different fonts.

We chose Old English (font #12), a font with the medieval style popularized by Kanye West's The Life of Pablo merchandise. With the titles given and font chosen, we were finished with the production of BDLA's Season I merchandise.
