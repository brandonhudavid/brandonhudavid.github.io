Ball Don't Lie Association

Brotherhood and brand embodying the spirit of the ball.
Established in Cerritos, California. December 2016.

" To maintain the unequivocal, ubiquitous revolution of the ball, we never consider ambivalence as a manifestation of our destiny, but rather as an opportunity; as a construction of our reality to reinforce and bolster the empire which has been given the grace of existence and the once clear notion that the ball has never, nor will ever, lie. "

A brotherhood.

What started as a dysfunctional group chat eventually became an inseparable bond between a faction of 14 Whitney High School Class of 2017 seniors. Whether it be performing a rendition of "Alexander Hamilton" in WHS's Talent Show, making multiple guest appearances on the WHS live show, or simply being the rowdiest crowd at every pep rally, the Ball Don't Lie Association grew in reputation throughout their last year of high school as they flaunted their light gray crewnecks and chanted:

"BALL DON'T LIE." -Rasheed Wallace

The association's name was derived from a famous basketball phrase popularized by former NBA player Rasheed Wallace. Often howled after controversial foul calls, the phrase attributes a player's, or even a team's, performance to the ball, representing a predetermined fate. In other words, "you get what you deserve."
